PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics)
PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)

MHT CET for PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics):

1.Physics (PCB):

a)Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electricity and Magnetism, Optics, Modern Physics.
b)Exam Weightage: Approximately 20-25% of the PCM paper.
c)Question Types: Conceptual understanding, numerical problems, and application-based questions.

2.Chemistry (PCM):

a)Physical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Organic Chemistry.
b)Exam Weightage: Around 20-25% of the PCM paper.
c)Question Types: Conceptual understanding, chemical equations, chemical bonding, periodic properties, organic reactions, etc.

3.Mathematics (PCM):

a)Algebra, Trigonometry, Calculus, Coordinate Geometry.
b)Exam Weightage: Highest weightage, approximately 50-60% of the PCM paper.
c)Question Types: Mathematical reasoning, problem-solving, calculus-based questions, algebraic manipulations, geometry problems.

MHT CET for PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology):

1)Physics (PCB):

a)Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electricity and Magnetism, Optics, Modern Physics.
b)Exam Weightage: Approximately 20-25% of the PCB paper.
c)Question Types: Similar to PCM physics, with a focus on biological applications and contexts.

2)Chemistry (PCB):

a)Physical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Organic Chemistry.
b)Exam Weightage: Around 20-25% of the PCB paper.
c)Question Types: Similar to PCM chemistry, with a focus on bio-molecules, biochemical reactions, and environmental chemistry.

3)Biology (PCB):

a)Botany and Zoology.
b)Botany: Plant Anatomy, Plant Physiology, Genetics, Ecology.
c)Zoology: Animal Diversity, Human Physiology, Cell Biology, Evolution, Genetics.
d)Exam Weightage: Highest weightage, approximately 50-60% of the PCB paper.
e)Question Types: Knowledge-based questions, application of concepts in biological systems, diagrams, and experimental questions.

Exam Pattern:

a)MHT CET is a multiple-choice question (MCQ) based exam.
b) Each subject (PCM or PCB) typically consists of 50 questions.
c)The total duration for each paper is 90 minutes.
d)There is no negative marking for incorrect answers.
Preparing for MHT CET requires a thorough understanding of the concepts, regular practice, and solving previous years' question papers. It's essential to manage time effectively during the exam and focus on accuracy.
