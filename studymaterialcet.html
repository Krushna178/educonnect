Here's a detailed breakdown of the study material you might need for the CET in India:

1)Syllabus

The first step is to understand the syllabus thoroughly. The CET syllabus typically covers subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology (depending on the course), and sometimes also includes subjects like English and Kannada.

2)Textbooks

Refer to standard textbooks recommended by education boards or institutions. NCERT textbooks are often a good starting point for subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. For Mathematics, textbooks by authors like R.D. Sharma or R.S. Aggarwal are popular choices. Make sure to choose books that align with the CET syllabus.

3)Previous Years' Question Papers

Solving previous years' question papers is crucial for understanding the exam pattern, types of questions asked, and time management. It also helps in identifying important topics and areas where you need more practice.

4)Practice Tests:

Take regular practice tests to assess your preparation level and improve your speed and accuracy. Many coaching institutes and online platforms provide mock tests specifically designed for CET preparation.

5)Reference Books:

Apart from textbooks, you may also refer to reference books for additional practice and in-depth understanding of concepts. Some popular reference books for CET preparation include:


1)Physics: Concepts of Physics by H.C. Verma, Fundamentals of Physics by Halliday, Resnick & Walker.
2)Chemistry: Organic Chemistry by Morrison & Boyd, Inorganic Chemistry by J.D. Lee, Physical Chemistry by O.P. Tandon.
3)Mathematics: Objective Mathematics by R.D. Sharma, Mathematics for Class 11 & 12 by R.S. Aggarwal.
4)Biology: Biology by Campbell & Reece, Trueman's Biology for NEET.

6)Online Resources

There are several websites and online platforms that offer study materials, video lectures, quizzes, and other resources for CET preparation. Make use of these resources to supplement your preparation.

7)Revision Notes:

Prepare concise notes covering important formulas, concepts, and key points for quick revision before the exam.

8)Coaching Institutes:

If you feel the need for additional guidance, you can consider joining a coaching institute that specializes in CET coaching. They often provide structured study material, classroom lectures, doubt clearing sessions, and mock tests to help students prepare effectively.

Remember to create a study schedule and stick to it consistently. Also, stay updated with any changes or notifications released by the exam conducting authority. Good luck with your CET preparation!
