For preparing for the Maharashtra Common Entrance Test (MHT-CET), it's essential to choose the right study materials and books. Here are some recommended books that cover the syllabus for MHT-CET:

1)Physics:

a)"Concepts of Physics" by H.C. Verma
b)"Fundamentals of Physics" by Halliday, Resnick, and Walker
c)"Problems in General Physics" by I.E. Irodov
d)"Physics for MHT-CET" by Nikita Publications
e)"Target MHT-CET (Medical & Engineering) Physics" by Disha Experts

2)Chemistry:

a)"Physical Chemistry" by O.P. Tandon
b)"Organic Chemistry" by Morrison & Boyd
c) "Inorganic Chemistry" by J.D. Lee
d)"Chemistry for MHT-CET" by Priyanka Prakashan
e)"Target MHT-CET (Medical & Engineering) Chemistry" by Disha Experts

3)Mathematics:

a)"Mathematics for Class 12" by R.D. Sharma
b)"Higher Algebra" by Hall and Knight
c)"Objective Mathematics" by R.D. Sharma
d)"Mathematics for MHT-CET" by Hemant G. Ainapure
e)"Target MHT-CET (Medical & Engineering) Mathematics" by Disha Experts

4)Biology (for PCB Group):

a)"NCERT Biology Textbooks for Class 11 and 12"
b)"Objective Biology" by Dinesh
c)"Biology for MHT-CET" by Sanjay O. Mehta
d)"Target MHT-CET (Medical & Engineering) Biology" by Disha Experts

5)General Knowledge:

a)"Lucent's General Knowledge" by Dr. Binay Karna and Manwendra Mukul
b)"Manorama Yearbook" by Mammen Mathew
c)"Encyclopedia of General Science" by Arihant Experts

These are some popular books that cover the syllabus comprehensively and provide ample practice questions. However, it's important to note that while these books are helpful, referring to your state board textbooks for concepts and theories specific to your syllabus is also crucial. Additionally, solving previous year papers and mock tests is highly recommended to understand the exam pattern and time management.
