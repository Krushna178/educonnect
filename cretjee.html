The JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) has various criteria associated with it, including eligibility criteria, exam pattern, and qualifying criteria. Here's an overview:

1)JEE Main:

a)Age Limit: There is no age limit for appearing in JEE Main.
b)Educational Qualification: Candidates must have passed or be appearing in the 10+2 examination or its equivalent with Physics and Mathematics as compulsory subjects along with one of Chemistry/Biotechnology/Biology/Technical Vocational subject.

2)JEE Advanced:

To be eligible for JEE Advanced, candidates must fulfill certain criteria including:
a)Performance in JEE Main: Candidates need to secure a position among the top candidates in JEE Main to be eligible for JEE Advanced.
b)Age Limit: The candidate should have been born on or after October 1, 1997. There is a relaxation in the upper age limit for reserved categories.
c)Number of Attempts: A candidate can attempt JEE Advanced a maximum of two times in consecutive years.

3)Exam Pattern:

a)JEE Main: The exam pattern for JEE Main typically consists of multiple-choice questions (MCQs) and numerical-based questions from Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. The exam can be conducted in either online (Computer Based Test) or offline (Pen and Paper Based Test) mode.

b)JEE Advanced: JEE Advanced has a different exam pattern compared to JEE Main. It usually consists of multiple-choice questions, numerical answer-type questions, and matching list questions. The exam is conducted in offline mode.

4)Qualifying Criteria:

a)JEE Main: The All India Rank (AIR) is determined based on the percentile score obtained in JEE Main. This rank is used for admission to various engineering colleges across India, including NITs, IIITs, and other GFTIs (Government Funded Technical Institutes).

b)JEE Advanced: The qualifying criteria for JEE Advanced involve securing a minimum percentage of marks in each subject and in aggregate in the qualifying examination (10+2 or equivalent). The exact cutoff marks vary depending on factors such as the difficulty level of the exam and the number of candidates appearing.

5)Reservation Criteria:

Both JEE Main and JEE Advanced have reservation criteria for candidates belonging to specific categories such as SC (Scheduled Caste), ST (Scheduled Tribe), OBC (Other Backward Classes), and PwD (Persons with Disability). These candidates are eligible for certain relaxations and benefits in terms of seat allocation and qualifying marks.

Understanding and fulfilling these criteria are essential for aspirants planning to appear for the JEE exams, as they determine eligibility for participation and admission to various engineering colleges and institutes in India.
